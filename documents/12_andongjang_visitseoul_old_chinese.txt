Andongjang / 안동장
Cuisine focus: old Seoul Chinese-Korean food.
Signature dish: oyster jjamppong / 굴짬뽕.
History context: Visit Seoul describes Andongjang as one of the oldest Chinese restaurants in Seoul and says it has kept classic tastes since 1948.

Expanded guide text:
Andongjang is important because Seoul’s local food history is not only “traditional Korean.” Chinese-Korean restaurants are part of modern Korean urban life, and old Chinese restaurants like Andongjang show how jjamppong, jajangmyeon, and banquet-style Chinese-Korean dishes became everyday Seoul food. Its oyster jjamppong makes it dish-specific rather than generic.

How to recommend:
- Recommend for travelers interested in old Seoul beyond barbecue and soup.
- Recommend for Euljiro / Jongno / central Seoul exploration.
- Mention that the key order is usually gul-jjamppong / oyster spicy noodle soup.
