Imun Seolnongtang / 이문설농탕 / 이문설렁탕
Cuisine focus: seolleongtang / 설렁탕.
Why it matters: One of the most important old restaurants in Seoul and Korea.

Korean source material:
Michelin Korean describes it as Seoul restaurant permit number one / 서울시 허가 제1호, a family restaurant that has operated through many historical moments. Korean cultural sources describe it as a 100+ year 설농탕 institution that boils beef parts for many hours to produce a milky, rich but clean soup. Seoul-local sources often frame it as “우리나라에서 가장 오래된 식당” or one of the oldest operating restaurants.

Expanded guide text:
This is exactly the kind of place the revised corpus should emphasize. Imun Seolnongtang is not trendy; its value is continuity. The dish is a milky beef-bone soup eaten with rice, green onion, salt/pepper adjustment, and kimchi. The experience teaches a visitor about Seoul’s old urban food culture: simple soup houses, fast nourishment, office workers, older patrons, and generational dining.

How to recommend:
- Recommend for old Seoul, historical restaurants, soup, breakfast/lunch, gentle flavors, and a pre/post Insadong or Jongno walk.
- Explain that 설렁탕 is often seasoned at the table; blandness can be intentional before adding salt, pepper, scallion, or kimchi.
- Ideal for users asking “what is the oldest restaurant in Seoul?” or “what should I eat near Insadong/Jongno?”
