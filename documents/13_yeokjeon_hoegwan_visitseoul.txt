Yeokjeon Hoegwan / 역전회관
Cuisine focus: bulgogi / 바싹불고기, house rice wine / 막걸리.
History context: Visit Seoul describes it as founded in 1929, third-generation, and about 97 years of tradition. It highlights crispy bulgogi grilled over charcoal using beef from Majang-dong and notes its house-made Yeokjeonju makgeolli.

Expanded guide text:
Yeokjeon Hoegwan is one of the strongest “old and renowned for a dish” sources in the pack. Bulgogi can be generic in tourist restaurants, but here the source points to a distinct house style: crispy/charcoal-grilled bulgogi rather than only saucy tabletop barbecue. It also fits a Mapo itinerary, which is useful because many travelers only know Hongdae/Yeonnam and miss older Mapo dining institutions.

How to recommend:
- Recommend for old Seoul bulgogi, Mapo, family meals, traditional alcohol pairing.
- Good alternative when the user wants meat but not a luxury hanwoo omakase.
- Pair with Gongdeok/Mapo drinking or a Han River evening.
