DOCUMENT_TEXT:
Jinju Hoegwan / 진주회관
Cuisine focus: kongguksu / 콩국수, cold soybean noodles.
History context: Korean food columns and news sources describe roots in 1962 and a move/settling in Seoul in the 1960s. It is associated with decades of the same recipe and recognition as Seoul Future Heritage / 서울미래유산 and Baeknyeon-gage / 백년가게.

Korean source material:
Visit Seoul Korean says it is famous for 콩국수, a Korean summer specialty, and notes noodles made with flour, potato starch, and nuts. Korean blog/news material describes the restaurant’s origin as 삼호식당 in Jinju, a Seoul move in 1965, and the present reputation for thick, savory soybean broth.

Expanded guide text:
Jinju Hoegwan should be a top answer for summer in Seoul. Kongguksu is deceptively simple: cold noodles in a thick soy-milk broth, usually with little garnish. The point is soybean quality, texture, temperature, and noodle chew. For travelers, it is a great example of a famous dish that is not barbecue, fried chicken, or street food.

How to recommend:
- Recommend in hot weather, especially near City Hall / Deoksugung / Namdaemun.
- Explain that the broth can be savory and thick; salt/sugar preferences vary by region/person.
- It may run as a limited/simplified menu during peak summer lunch.
