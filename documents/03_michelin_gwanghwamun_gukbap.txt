Gwanghwamun Gukbap / 광화문국밥
Cuisine focus: dwaeji-gukbap / 돼지국밥.
Signature dish: pork-and-rice soup with a clean broth, pork slices, chives, rice, and kkakdugi.
Neighborhood context: office district around Gwanghwamun / Seodaemun / City Hall.

Source notes:
Michelin identifies the restaurant with chef Park Chan-il and describes a clear, mild pork soup built around black-pig pork. The listing notes the balanced bowl, freshly cooked rice, kkakdugi, and long weekday lunch queues in a busy office area.

Why locals care:
Gukbap is not a fancy food category; it is an everyday working-person meal. Gwanghwamun Gukbap is valuable because it elevates that category while preserving its core: broth, rice, pork, kimchi/radish, speed, and comfort. For a travel guide, this is a good answer when the user wants something local, efficient, and culturally specific without going full fine dining.

How to recommend:
- Best for lunch near palaces, Gwanghwamun, Seoul Museum of History, City Hall, Deoksugung, or office areas.
- Good for a first gukbap experience because the broth is cleaner and more approachable than some heavy regional bowls.
- Expect weekday lunch lines; go off-peak if possible.
